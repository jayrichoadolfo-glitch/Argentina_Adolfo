Home Page

Visiting artisanal chocolate shops that have earned the city the nickname "the Little Switzerland of Argentina."

Up in Bariloche, the chocolate industry is a beautiful example of 'post-war migration' influence, where Italian techniques met Patagonian berries.

Post-WWII, Italian chocolatiers noticed the Patagonian climate was similar to the Alps. They started mixing European techniques with Rosehip and Calafate berry.

What to Expect

Huge shop windows with chocolate "waterfalls" and unique flavors like chocolate with Patagonian berries or Dulce de Leche.

The Recipe (Submarino)

Drop a high-quality bar of dark chocolate into a glass of steaming hot milk. Stir until the emulsifiers break down and you get the thickest hot chocolate of your life.
