Argentina is more than just a destination; it is a collection of vast, contrasting landscapes and deep-rooted traditions that span from the tropical heat of the north to the "End of the World" in the south. Whether you are chasing the thunder of glacial ice, dancing the night away in a dimly lit tango hall, or sipping world-class Malbec at the foot of the Andes, there is a rhythm here for every traveler.

Before you pack your bags, use this guide to explore the four pillars of Argentine tourism. From the history of the explorers who first mapped these lands to the sensory details of what to expect on the ground, we’ve gathered everything you need to know to transform your trip into a true immersion.

Pro Tips

The "Blue" Dollar

Make sure to research the 'Dollar Blue'—Argentina has a unique dual-exchange rate system that can make your trip much more affordable if you bring cash!

The Dinner Clock

Don't head to a restaurant at 7:00 PM; you'll find it empty! Argentines typically eat dinner between 9:00 PM and 11:00 PM.

The Mate Ritual

If someone offers you Mate (a bitter herbal tea), it’s a sign of friendship. Drink it through the metal straw, and never stir it!

Voltage Check

Argentina uses Type I plugs (three flat pins in a V-shape) and 220V. Check your electronics before you plug in!
